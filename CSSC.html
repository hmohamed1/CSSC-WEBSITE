What to Wear

Essential Gear

  • Thick, warm socks (boots can be uncomfortable)
  • Waterproof outer shell or jacket
  • Waterproof gloves or mittens
  • Thermal base layers
  • Insulating mid-layer (fleece or wool)
  • Helmet (safety first!)
  • Goggles

Pro Tips

  • Layer up! It's easier to remove layers if you get too warm.
  • Avoid cotton - it retains moisture and can make you cold.
  • Don't forget sunscreen and lip balm - the sun reflects off the snow!

Skiing vs Snowboarding

Skiing

  • Easier to learn initially
  • Can be challenging to master advanced techniques
  • You can typically enjoy going down the hill on your first day

Snowboarding

  • Can be more challenging to learn at first
  • Once you get the basics, progress can be quicker
  • Expect to spend most of your first day learning the fundamental stance and movements

Remember, both skiing and snowboarding are fantastic ways to enjoy the winter. Choose whichever appeals to you more, and don't be afraid to try both!

Beginner's Corner

New to winter sports? Don't worry! Here are some tips to help you get started:

  • Take a lesson - it's the fastest way to learn proper technique and safety
  • Start on gentle slopes and progress gradually
  • Practice falling safely - it's part of the learning process!
  • Stay hydrated and take breaks when needed
  • Most importantly, have fun and don't be too hard on yourself!
